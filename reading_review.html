    <div class="chapter">

        <h2>Chapter 1: What is Safeguarding?</h2>

        <p>Maya was introduced to the concept of <strong> safeguarding</strong> when she attended a workshop at the community center. She learned that safeguarding meant protecting citizens' <strong>health</strong>, <strong>well-being</strong>, and <strong>human rights</strong>, enabling them to live free from <strong>harm</strong>, <strong>abuse</strong>, and <strong>neglect</strong>. The training opened her eyes to how vulnerable groups—<strong>children</strong>, <strong>young people</strong>, and <strong>adults at risk</strong>—needed special protection.</p>

    </div>

   

    <div class="chapter">

        <h2>Chapter 2: Safeguarding in Domestic Violence (DV) and Violence Against Women and Girls (VAWG) Campaigns</h2>

        <p>Maya soon found herself working on a campaign aimed at preventing <strong>domestic violence</strong> and <strong>violence against women and girls</strong> (VAWG). She learned that <strong>VAWG</strong> wasn’t just one form of abuse—it was a term that encompassed <strong>rape</strong>, <strong>sexual assault</strong>, <strong>child sexual abuse</strong>, <strong>domestic abuse</strong>, and so much more.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="chapter">

        <h2>Chapter 3: The Importance of Respecting Children’s Rights</h2>

        <p>One day, Maya was asked to create a campaign highlighting the <strong>rights</strong> and <strong>dignity</strong> of <strong>children</strong>. She was reminded of a key truth: <strong>children’s dignity</strong> must always be respected. Children are especially vulnerable to <strong>harm</strong> and <strong>exploitation</strong>, so Maya made sure that every campaign message she crafted respected their <strong>intrinsic value</strong>.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="chapter">

        <h2>Chapter 4: Key Principles for Reporting on Children and Young People</h2>

        <p>As Maya learned more about <strong>responsible reporting</strong>, she understood the significance of the <strong>principles</strong> that guided ethical communication, especially when working with children and young people. She learned that the <strong>dignity and rights</strong> of every child must be respected at all times. She was reminded to be mindful of their <strong>privacy</strong> and <strong>confidentiality</strong> to avoid harm or retribution.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="chapter">

        <h2>Chapter 5: The Role of GEC Communication for Change</h2>

        <p>Maya then encountered the <strong>Girls’ Education Challenge</strong> (GEC) <strong>Communication for Change</strong> guidance, which laid out the framework for creating communication materials that were <strong>safe</strong> and <strong>effective</strong> in promoting social change. Maya learned that <strong>positive role models</strong>, <strong>accessible language</strong>, and <strong>child-friendly</strong> materials were key in empowering survivors.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="chapter">

        <h2>Chapter 6: Effective Communication Strategies</h2>

        <p>As Maya grew more experienced, she became involved in <strong>Information Education Communication (IEC)</strong>, <strong>Behavior Change Communication (BCC)</strong>, and <strong>Behavior Change Intervention (BCI)</strong>. She learned that <strong>IEC</strong> provided essential <strong>information</strong> to help survivors make <strong>informed decisions</strong>.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="chapter">

        <h2>Chapter 7: Best Practices for Ethical Communication</h2>

        <p>In her journey, Maya also learned best practices for <strong>ethical communication</strong>. She realized that for a message to be effective, it needed to involve the community. <strong>Women</strong>, <strong>men</strong>, and <strong>youth</strong> had to be part of the conversation, and <strong>culturally relevant</strong> messages needed to resonate with the local values.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="chapter">

        <h2>Chapter 8: Safeguarding on Social Media</h2>

        <p>As the digital world evolved, Maya became more conscious of how to navigate <strong>social media</strong> safely. She learned that communication on these platforms had to follow a <strong>safeguarding policy</strong>. This meant protecting the <strong>dignity</strong> of beneficiaries, ensuring <strong>consent</strong> was obtained from both children and parents before sharing any stories or images.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="chapter">

        <h2>Chapter 9: A Case Study - Protecting Lina’s Story</h2>

        <p>One day, Maya’s team received a report about <strong>Lina</strong>, a young girl who had experienced <strong>verbal abuse</strong> at home. Maya’s team wanted to raise awareness by sharing her story, but they knew they had to do so with care. They made sure not to reveal Lina’s personal details. Instead, they focused on raising awareness about the <strong>dangerous cycle of abuse</strong>.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="chapter">

        <h2>Chapter 10: Maya’s Reflection</h2>

        <p>Through her work, Maya learned the immense responsibility that comes with communicating stories involving survivors of <strong>violence</strong> and <strong>abuse</strong>. She realized that her role was not just to share information but to protect the rights of those whose voices were often silenced.</p>

        let currentChapter = 0;

        const chapters = document.querySelectorAll('.chapter');

        function showChapter(index) {

            chapters.forEach((chapter, i) => {

                chapter.style.display = i === index ? 'block' : 'none';

            });

        }

        function nextChapter() {

            if (currentChapter < chapters.length - 1) {

                currentChapter++;

                showChapter(currentChapter);

            }

        }

        function previousChapter() {

            if (currentChapter > 0) {

                currentChapter--;

                showChapter(currentChapter);

            }

        }

        // Initialize the first chapter to show

        showChapter(currentChapter);
